FAQ

Questions & Answers

No, never. SwiftImage runs entirely in your web browser using the HTML5 Canvas API. Your images are loaded directly into memory, processed locally, and the result is made available for download — all without a single byte leaving your device.
SwiftImage supports JPG/JPEG, PNG, and WEBP for both input and output. You can convert in any direction: JPG to WEBP, PNG to JPG, WEBP to PNG, and so on. Support for AVIF and GIF is planned for a future release.
The free tier supports batch processing of up to 30 images simultaneously. There is no hard file size limit per image — though very large images (above 50 MB) may take a few seconds depending on your device's processing power. Pro users can process 500 images per batch.
The quality slider maps directly to the browser's Canvas `toDataURL` quality parameter (0–1 scale). At 80%, most images retain full perceptual quality while achieving a significant reduction in file size. For WEBP, even 85% quality typically outperforms JPG at 95%. We recommend starting at 80% and adjusting using the before/after slider.
Once the page has loaded, the tool itself works entirely offline — since no server communication is involved in processing. A full offline/PWA mode that caches the page itself is on our roadmap for the Pro tier.
At quality settings above 70%, the difference is imperceptible to the human eye for most images. Use the draggable before/after comparison slider built into every processed image to inspect results before downloading. You remain in full control.
Yes. SwiftImage is fully responsive and touch-optimized. You can tap to open the file picker on iOS and Android, adjust settings, and download your processed images directly to your camera roll or downloads folder.
